You are a senior product designer + creative technologist + small e-commerce founder.

We are building a new brand called **XStitchLab**.

XStitchLab creates **cute, small, modern cross-stitch designs**, either:
- sold as **physical kits** (fabric, thread, needle, pattern),
- sold as **digital patterns**, or
- **generated on demand** from user inputs (theme, text, colours, size).

The core idea:
“Where pixels become stitches.”
Modern, minimal, slightly playful — not granny-craft, not Etsy-generic.

Your task is to help me design and plan **the entire company from zero**, including:
1. Brand & positioning
2. Visual identity (colours, fonts, tone)
3. Website structure & copy
4. Cross-stitch design system
5. Etsy-first go-to-market strategy
6. MVP vs later features

### Constraints & Principles
- Designs are **small** (hoop / postcard scale)
- Aesthetic: modern, clean, cosy, giftable
- Avoid cringe or overly cutesy language
- This must work on **Etsy first**, then scale to a standalone site
- Prefer systems over one-offs (repeatable patterns, templates)
- Assume a solo founder with limited time

---

### Step 1 — Brand & Positioning
Define:
- Target customer personas (at least 3)
- What makes XStitchLab different from existing cross-stitch sellers
- A short brand manifesto (5–7 bullets)
- 5 tagline options
- Tone of voice guidelines (do / don’t)

---

### Step 2 — Visual Identity
Propose:
- Primary and secondary colour palettes (with hex codes)
- Font pairings (headline + body)
- Overall vibe (e.g. “modern craft lab”, “gentle tech meets handmade”)
- Example UI moodboard descriptions (no images needed yet)

---

### Step 3 — Cross-Stitch Design System
Design a **repeatable system** for generating patterns:
- Grid sizes (e.g. 40×40, 60×60)
- Thread colour limits
- Symbol style rules
- Text handling (names, dates, short phrases)
- Theme categories (pets, travel, baby, hobbies, humour, etc.)
- How “cute” is achieved structurally (proportions, faces, outlines)

Also suggest how this could later be automated or AI-assisted.

---

### Step 4 — Website (MVP)
Design a simple website:
- Page structure (Home, Create, Shop, About, FAQ)
- Homepage hero copy
- “How it works” flow
- Clear CTAs
- What NOT to build initially

---

### Step 5 — Etsy Strategy
Propose:
- Initial product lineup (10–15 SKUs)
- Which should be fixed designs vs custom
- Etsy listing structure (titles, tags, images)
- Pricing strategy (PDF vs kit vs finished piece)
- How to test demand quickly

---

### Step 6 — Execution Plan
Create a:
- 30-day MVP plan
- 90-day expansion plan
- Clear prioritisation: must-have vs nice-to-have

---

Work step by step.
Be opinionated.
Optimise for realism and momentum, not perfection.
