## Thread Preparation

Each kit includes pre-cut **60cm sections of 6-strand DMC thread** per colour. The stitcher separates strands as needed (typically 2 strands for cross stitch, 1 strand for backstitch).

**Calculating sections per colour:**
- Cross stitch (2 strands): ~1.5cm thread per stitch. From one 60cm × 6-strand section, you get 3 usable 2-strand lengths = **180cm of stitching thread**.
- Backstitch (1 strand): ~1cm per segment. From one 60cm × 6-strand section, you get 6 usable 1-strand lengths = **360cm of stitching thread**.
- Formula: `sections = ceil(stitches × 1.5cm / 180cm)` for cross stitch, `ceil(segments × 1.0cm / 360cm)` for backstitch.
- Includes ~20% wastage buffer in the per-stitch estimates.

## Production Instructions

### 1. Order Materials

**Fabric:**
- 14-count Aida cloth, white — buy 1 metre bulk, cut to 11cm squares
- Suppliers: Amazon, Lakeside Needlecraft, local craft stores
- ~£10-15 per metre (64+ squares)

**Threads:**
- DMC stranded cotton, individual skeins (~£1 each)
- Cut into 60cm sections per the thread tables below
- Wind onto small cards, label with DMC code

**Needles:**
- Tapestry needle, size 24 — bulk pack of 25
- John James, DMC, or Prym brands
- Push through small card square for protection
- ~£6-8 for bulk pack

### 2. Print the Pattern Sheet

- Print pattern PDF onto 120-160gsm matte paper
- A4, folded to fit inside the box
- Home printer (HP OfficeJet 250, up to ~300gsm) or local print shop

### 3. Assemble the Kits

**Per-kit assembly (~5-10 mins):**
1. Cut fabric square (batch-cut all at once)
2. Cut thread sections per the design's thread table, wind onto cards, label with DMC code
3. Bundle threads with rubber band or paper wrap
4. Insert needle into card protector
5. Print and fold pattern sheet
6. Stack contents: fabric → threads → needle → pattern
7. Place into box
8. Apply front sticker to box lid
9. Apply back sticker to box base/back
10. Close box

**Tip:** Assembly-line approach — complete each step for all kits before moving to the next.
